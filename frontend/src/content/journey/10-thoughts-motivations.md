## My Philosophy

✨ Embracing Change for Personal Growth ✨

Reflecting on my journey, I used to immerse myself in motivational quotes and videos whenever I hit a low point. However, I realized that despite the inspiration, I wasn't implementing these lessons in my life. It's easy to get lost in the multitude of advice and struggle to commit to a single change.

Growing up, I unintentionally followed this pattern, never truly committing to any transformation. Recently, I had an awakening about this cycle and decided to break free from it. I made a conscious choice to stop merely reading quotes and, instead, vowed to implement every insight into my daily life.

That decision led me to a profound quote on LinkedIn that resonated deeply with me: 

" Without commitment, you will never start..
without consistency, you will never finish.. "

 It struck a chord, and I made a firm commitment to apply this principle in my life.

Today, I encourage you to reflect on your own journey. Are you simply consuming motivational content, or are you actively incorporating those lessons into your life? Let's make a collective effort to not just start, but to commit and stay consistent on our paths to personal growth. Remember, it's through commitment and consistency that we truly bring about lasting change. 💪✨

From Dreams to Decisions: The Story Behind My First Home, Car & iPhone 🏠🚗📱
When I started my first job, I was deeply fascinated by my colleagues’ iPhones, Royal Enfield bikes, and cars. As an automobile enthusiast since childhood, I often imagined owning one myself. With some savings finally appearing in my account, I began planning to buy a Used Car and an iPhone.
I had complete freedom — my parents never restricted my choices — yet something inside held me back. One day, it struck me: we were still living in a rented house and had never owned a home.
That thought changed everything.
My parents had invested their lifetime savings to buy a 1,100 sq. ft. plot, conduct my sister’s marriage, and support my education. I was able to complete my graduation with a fee reimbursement facility and had no plans for further studies.
The very next day, I decided to apply for my first-ever bank loan — a home loan — to build a house for my parents on that land they had bought years ago. The day construction started remains one of the most fulfilling moments of my life.
Years later, with steady career growth and salary hikes, I finally bought a New Car for my family and an iPhone for myself, achieving two long-awaited dreams — 5 years and 7 years in the making.
No one guided me through this journey. I simply followed my instincts and a bit of common sense — to prioritize what truly matters before pursuing desires.
It taught me one important life lesson:
Fulfillment doesn’t come from what we own, but from what we build for the people we love. ❤️

Recently, I have cleared the AWS AI Practitioner Certification.
I followed a simple process to prepare for this certification exam.
Finding the time for preparation and committing to that time regularly is difficult in a typical busy week, managing work-life balance.
I go to the office in a hybrid mode, 3 days a week, and it takes me two hours of travel time per day. I started spending those two hours of travel time when I’m working from home for the certification preparation. With this, I managed to spend 4 hours a week on weekdays without sacrificing anything in my routine and managed to spend 2-3 hours more on weekends.

I set this process and utilised this time mainly to learn new AI concepts that are necessary for certification, taking notes and refining them to post on LinkedIn so that others may benefit from it. I posted about 50+ posts on the concepts/topics that I learnt/prepared for the certification every week consistently, which helped me to revise the concepts whenever I needed and understand those concepts in another way.

This certification and setting up the process for preparation and maintaining that consistency is an example of commitment to something. All you need to do is set the process and believe in it.

I enjoyed this process rather than clearing the certification exam; you should believe in your process, whether you succeed or fail, the result doesn’t matter.

This certification gave me confidence in the process that I tried for the first time.
I can set and apply a similar process for other things, like upskilling, another certification, a workout, etc.. without worrying about the result, as I always believe in the process that I set.

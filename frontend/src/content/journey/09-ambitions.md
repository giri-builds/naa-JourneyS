## Looking Ahead

My short-term ambition is to land in to a AI Engineer role with good CTC by the end of the year 2026.
Get out of the EMI's by end of 2030 or have minimum of less than 40% of your salary.

Career aspiration is to do the M.tech from IIT Madras in AI/ML 
Start a company in home town to help the under privileged talented students to achieve their goals and land in their dream career


When I was a kid, I dreamed of becoming a Police Officer.
 My father often spoke about the bravery of officers — especially Umesh Chandra, IPS — and those stories left a deep impression on me. I imagined myself wearing that uniform one day.
But dreams alone aren’t enough. I had no idea how to become an IPS officer — no one to guide me, and I never asked my teachers either. Deep down, I also knew my parents couldn’t afford the expenses for such preparation.
So, like many others around me, I followed the crowd.
 My sister casually mentioned that most students were taking M.P.C. and doing Engineering. I did the same — without questioning whether it aligned with my dream.
🎓 Fast forward to my final year of Engineering — our college didn’t have campus placements, and reality hit hard. I suddenly remembered my childhood dream and started exploring police recruitment exams.
I applied for the Indian Air Force, cleared the written test with limited preparation, and got shortlisted for the SSB interview.
 That was my first-ever interview. I travelled to Mysore, stayed overnight at the railway station, and reported at 6 AM sharp — only to find hundreds of talented candidates from all over India.
I gave my best, but I wasn’t confident enough.
 I struggled during group discussions and was ultimately rejected. That was my first failure.
Soon after, the Andhra Pradesh Government released the Sub-Inspector (SI) recruitment notification in 2011, the same year I graduated. I saw it as a second chance.
I trained hard for the physical tests — 5 km run, 100 m sprint, 800 m run, long jump, high jump, and shot put. I completed the 5 km run in 20 minutes (against the 25-minute limit) and scored merit in events.
Now, all that stood between me and my dream was the final written test —

Paper 1: Arithmetic & Reasoning ✅ (my strong area)

Paper 2: General Studies ❌ (my weak area — History, Geography, Economy, Current Affairs)

For the first time, the exam introduced negative marking. I did well in Paper 1 but struggled in Paper 2. When the results came, I fell 10 marks short — only because of negative marking.
If not for that change, I might have been wearing the uniform I once dreamed of.
That was my second failure.
I had already spent two years after graduation preparing for this goal. The next notification was delayed due to the Telangana bifurcation, and I couldn’t afford to wait any longer. My parents needed financial support, so I took a different path — IT.
I later realized the real mistake wasn’t failure — it was not knowing the right path early enough.
 If I had spoken to a Police Officer or career counsellor, I would’ve known that to become an SI, it’s better to take H.E.C. in 12th and pursue a B.A. degree (History, Economics, Civics, Political Science, Sociology).
I learned it the hard way — too late to apply it.
Today, I’m happy with my job, my growth, and my career in IT. But every time I see a Police Officer, I can’t help but remember that boyhood dream and the mistakes that shaped my journey.
To every student or youngster reading this 👇
Don’t just follow the crowd.
Don’t choose a path because everyone else does.
Ask questions, seek guidance, and learn early what it takes to reach your goal.
Your dreams deserve clarity — not confusion.
And remember: failure doesn’t define you — lack of direction does.
